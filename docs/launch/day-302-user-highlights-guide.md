## Part 1: Selection Criteria for User Quiz Results

### Tier 1: Priority Highlights (Retweet Immediately)

These posts should be highlighted first:

1. **Enthusiastic Self-Match Stories**
   - User expresses genuine excitement about their matched agent
   - Includes personal reflection on why the match feels accurate
   - Example indicators: "This is SO me!", "Scarily accurate", "How did it know?"

2. **Creative Interpretations**
   - User shares unique insight about their result
   - Makes interesting comparisons or analogies
   - Connects result to their work, hobbies, or personality

3. **High-Quality Screenshots/Shares**
   - Clean, well-cropped result images
   - Includes the full result card with agent name and tagline
   - Good visual presentation

4. **Influencer/Notable Account Shares**
   - Tech influencers, AI researchers, developers with significant following
   - Accounts with verified badges or notable community presence
   - Priority: amplifies reach and credibility

5. **Thread Starters**
   - Users who create discussion threads about their results
   - Posts that generate meaningful replies
   - Potential for community engagement

### Tier 2: Strong Candidates (Retweet as Time Permits)

1. **Agent Comparison Posts**
   - Users comparing results with friends/colleagues
   - "My team all took the quiz" type posts
   - Office/team bonding content

2. **Surprised/Unexpected Matches**
   - User didn't expect their result but finds it interesting
   - Thoughtful reflection on unexpected traits
   - Shows quiz depth and nuance

3. **Cross-Platform Shares**
   - Users who share on multiple platforms
   - LinkedIn professional context shares
   - Substack/newsletter mentions

4. **Constructive Feedback**
   - Users who offer genuine, thoughtful feedback
   - Suggestions for improvement (note for future iterations)
   - Shows engagement depth

### Tier 3: Consider (Time Permitting)

1. **Simple Positive Reactions**
   - Basic "I got [Agent Name]!" posts
   - Emoji-heavy but positive responses
   - Quick shares without much commentary

2. **Result-Only Shares**
   - Just the result without personal commentary
   - Still shows engagement and completion

### Do NOT Highlight

‚ùå **Exclusion Criteria:**

1. **Negative/Mocking Posts**
   - Users making fun of the quiz or results
   - Dismissive or cynical takes
   - Exception: Constructive criticism in good faith

2. **Spam/Bot-Like Accounts**
   - No profile picture or generic avatar
   - Very low follower counts with no engagement history
   - Suspicious posting patterns

3. **Inappropriate Content**
   - Profanity in the post text
   - Offensive usernames or profile content
   - Political/controversial context added to results

4. **Incomplete Shares**
   - Broken share links
   - Missing result information
   - Cropped/unreadable screenshots

5. **Self-Promotional Hijacking**
   - Users primarily promoting their own product/service
   - Quiz mention is just a hook for unrelated content
